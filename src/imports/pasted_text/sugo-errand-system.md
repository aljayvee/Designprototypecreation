Purpose and Description
This study aimed to address the operational inefficiencies of Sugo Services On the Go caused by messenger-based coordination, unautomated financial calculations, and fragmented information management. The study focused on developing a Mobile-based Errand Service Management System that would transition the business from informal chat-based operations to a structured digital platform with automated processes and centralized data management. The following are the functions of the system:
1)	Errand Transaction Management
a)	The system records specific service types including Pabili, Padala, and Bills Payment with distinct workflow requirements for each category. -Wrong
b)	The system enables dispatchers to input customer details, delivery addresses, and specific landmarks.
c)	The system manages payment mode recording for Cash on Delivery, GCash, and Bank Transfers with automated flags for high-value transactions 3,000 pesos.
d)	The system integrates a photo verification feature where riders upload original store receipts and item photographs.
e)	The system allows riders to update errand statuses in real time including arrival at store, item purchased, en route to delivery, and order delivered.
2)	Rate Management
a)	The system should automatically compute the total bill based on distance-calculated base delivery fees.
b)	The system should apply the multiple store rule by automatically adding a 25-peso surcharge for each additional store beyond the first.
c)	The system should accurately calculate grocery commissions by applying a ten percent fee for purchases above one thousand pesos or a 50-peso flat fee for purchases.
d)	The system includes a verification gate for non-Cash on Delivery transactions involving high-value items above three thousand pesos.
3)	Rider Management
a)	The system tracks and displays the real-time status of thirty-eight riders as Available, On an Errand, or Offline.
b)	The system generates digital waybills for riders containing structured address information, customer contact details, and specific landmarks to reduce navigation.
c)	The system manages the assignment of errands to available riders through a centralized dispatch interface.
4)	Reports Management
a)	Sales Report
i)	The system should show daily transactions totals and breakdown service type.
ii)	The system should display weekly aggregated trends and monthly comprehensive summaries.
iii)	The system should display the peak hour analysis and revenue distributions in different errand categories.
b)	Rider Performance Report
i)	The system should list the individual rider’s grades by their completed errand task
ii)	The system should display the completed errand task count by the riders.
iii)	The system should display the completion of the assignments and average delivery time.
iv)	The system should document repeat customer interactions and incident logs.
v)	The system should calculate the rates against the assignment task given to the rider.
c)	Commission Report
i)	The system should provide a detail breakdown of errand task such as commission.
ii)	The system should distinguish between 10% variable commission to 50 pesos flat fees.
iii)	The system should list all the multiple surcharges applied with the transaction references.
iv)	The system should summarize the total commission revenue generated by the rider per time period.
d)	Errand Settlement Report
i)	The system should automatically calculate the end-of-day settlement process by calculating total service fees collected
ii)	The system should include the itemizing all commissions earned from grocery errands.
iii)	The system should display the multiple store surcharges and the final Sugo Share allocations for business revenue.
e)	Transaction Summary Reports
i)	The system should produce an automated daily statement of total business capital.
ii)	The system should calculate the total revenue from service fees and commission.
5)	Notification Management
a)	The system provides a waybill information locally on rider devices, ensuring access in barangays with inconsistent internet connectivity.
b)	The system sends automated confirmations to customers when a rider is dispatched, when items are purchased
6)	Customer Self-service Management
a)	The system should provide an option to order an item.
b)	The system should create a custom service order.
c)	The system should generate a digital order slip.
d)	The system should provide an interface for tracing the rider’s location.
Upon implementation, the system can benefit the following stakeholders:
1.	Business Owner – The system can help the owner to monitor the entire operation through a centralized dashboard showing real-time rider locations, active errand status, and accurate financial summaries. It provides performance data and revenue trends that support strategic decisions regarding fleet expansion, service area extension, and rate adjustments. 
2.	Dispatchers – The system can help dispatchers perform their tasks more efficiently by replacing unautomated message relaying with structured digital forms and automated customer record retrieval. It streamlines the order intake process through standardized input fields and eliminates the daily administrative burden of scrolling through chat history to verify customer identity or locate previous delivery addresses.
3.	Delivery Personnel – The system can support riders by providing clear structured instructions and landmarks through digital waybills that reduce navigation errors and delivery delays. It automates commission and surcharge calculations, ensuring accurate fee totals without requiring unautomated computation during the procurement phase. 
4.	Customers – The system can provide customers with a more reliable service experience through automated status updates at key transaction points rather than requiring them to message for progress inquiries. It ensures accurate billing through automated fee calculation engines that apply consistent rates regardless of which rider handles the errand.
5.	Future Researchers – This study can serve as a reference for developers and researchers creating similar automated management systems for localized logistics and concierge businesses in the Philippines. It documents the transition process from informal messenger-based coordination to structured mobile platforms, providing insights into operational challenges specific to small and medium errand service enterprises. 
Objectives
Generally, this study aimed to develop a Mobile-based Errand Service Management System for Sugo Service On the Go to automate errand tracking and financial report. Specifically, this study aimed to accomplish the following specific objectives:
1)	Backend Modules
a)	Dashboard Module - Display analytics regarding total daily errands completed, total active number riders, real-time income summaries showing accumulated service fees throughout the day.
b)	Errand GPS Management Module – Displays active rider locations using their internal GPS coordinates.
c)	User Management Module - Enable the administrator to create and manage user accounts with distinct roles for the business owner, dispatchers, and the thirty-eight riders. 
d)	Rider Management Module – This allows the availability of riders and the status when doing task, generate waybills containing the structured address and customer full name.
e)	Record Management Module - Enable the dispatcher to record new customer information including full name, primary contact number, complete delivery address, and specific recurring landmarks such as nearby stores, color of gate, or street identifiers.
f)	Rate Management Module - Allow the administrator to configure service types including Pabili, Padala, and Bills Payment with distinct workflow requirements. 
g)	Dispatch Management Module - Provide a centralized digital queue displaying all incoming errand requests with status indicators for pending, assigned, in progress, and completed. 
h)	Notification Management Module – This module notifies the details of the rider’s information about its current location. Additionally, send an automated confirmations to customers when a rider is dispatched and when the riders arrive at the delivery location.
i)	Customer-self Service Module – This module handles the customer order and request in the app to interact with the dispatcher. Moreover, it will show the location of the rider when the request has been done. Additionally, this module should handle the payment in the Pabili, Padala, and Bills Payment.
j)	Report Management Module - Generates the following reports:
i)	Sales Report - Showing daily transaction totals with breakdown by service type, weekly aggregated revenue trends with comparison to previous periods, and monthly comprehensive summaries including peak hour analysis and revenue distribution across different errand categories.
ii)	Rider Performance Report - Listing individual riders ranked by completed errand count, successful delivery completion rate calculated against attempted assignments, average delivery time from assignment to completion, customer frequency metrics showing repeat customer interactions, and incident logs documenting failed deliveries or payment disputes.
iii)	Commission Log - Providing detailed breakdown of every grocery errand commission calculated, distinguishing between ten percent variable commissions and 50-peso flat fees, listing all multiple store surcharges applied with transaction references, and summarizing total commission revenue generated per rider and per time period.
iv)	Errand Settlement Module - Automate the end-of-day settlement process by calculating total service fees collected, itemizing all commissions earned from grocery errands, summarizing multiple store surcharges, and determining the final Sugo Share allocation for business revenue versus Rider Share allocation for driver earnings. 
v)	Transaction Summary Reports - producing an automated daily statement of total business capital deployed for customer purchases, total revenue from service fees and commissions, net Sugo Share after deducting rider payments and operational costs, and individual rider earnings statements with itemized base fees and incentive calculations.
2)	Frontend Modules
a)	Errand Transaction Module - Enable riders to view assigned errands with complete customer details and digital waybill information. This module allows acceptance or declination of task assignments with reason logging, updating of errand status progression from arrival at pickup location through item procurement to final delivery, capture and upload of store receipt photographs with automatic timestamp and map verification, recording of item purchase costs for reimbursement tracking, and confirmation of payment collection with method specification and amount entry.
b)	Rate and Commission Module - Automatically display calculated fee totals based on the items purchased and stores visited. This module shows the base delivery fee determined by distance, adds the 25-peso surcharge for each additional store beyond the first.
c)	Customer-self Service Module – Automatically create a digital order slip and digital receipt in the system. Moreover, it should accept a payment, location interface, status of the rider’s location, and done task.
Scope and Limitations
This study aimed to develop a Mobile-based Errand Service Management System to address the problem of unautomated fleet coordination and financial reporting at Sugo Services On the Go and help improve their business performance. The target users of the system are the owner John Dominic Arellano, dispatchers, and the active delivery riders. The study was conducted in the second semester of the school year 2025-2026. The following is the scope of the system:
1)	Backend Modules
a)	The Dashboard Module - It displays analytics about daily errand volume with hourly breakdown showing peak transaction periods, active rider status with available, on errand, and offline indicators, financial summary showing accumulated revenue throughout the business day with real-time updates as transactions complete, and comparative charts displaying weekly and monthly performance trends.
b)	The User Management Module – This provides functionality to assign user accounts according to distinct roles for Owner, Dispatcher, and Rider. This module includes functionalities to add new users with complete profile information including contact details and vehicle assignments, update existing profiles when riders change phone numbers or motorcycle registrations, and deactivate or activate accounts when riders take extended leave or return to active duty.
c)	Record Management Module – This records all customer information such as full name, primary and secondary contact numbers, complete delivery address with barangay and municipality identifiers, ID and liveness verification through call indicating whether the customer has completed previous transactions without issues, and specific delivery landmarks including nearby commercial establishments, house colors, or street identifiers to minimize navigation delays for riders unfamiliar with the area.
d)	The Rate Management Module - This enables the manager to add and update service types including Pabili for purchasing errands, Padala for document and package delivery, and Bills Payment for utility bill settlement. This module manages service rates including base delivery fees calculated by distance brackets within Tacurong City and extending to neighboring municipalities such as Lambayong and Isulan, the 25-peso surcharge rule for multiple store visits, and the grocery commission structure with ten percent for purchases above one thousand pesos and 50-peso flat fee for smaller orders.
e)	The Dispatch Management Module – This provides the status of the incoming errand request and provides real-time rider availability of every rider in the city. This module enables the dispatcher to view available rider and its status real-time.
f)	Notification Management Module – This module notifies the details of the rider’s information about its current location. Additionally, send an automated confirmations to customers when a rider is dispatched and when the riders arrive at the delivery location.
g)	Customer-self Service Module – This module handles the customer order and request in the app to interact with the dispatcher. Moreover, it will show the location of the rider when the request has been done. Additionally, this module should handle the payment in the Pabili, Padala, and Bills Payment.
h)	The Report Management Module provides functionality to generate periodic reports such as:
i)	Sales Report – Showing daily transaction totals with itemized breakdown of service fees, commission earnings, and surcharge revenues, weekly aggregated summaries comparing current performance against previous weeks with percentage change indicators, and monthly comprehensive statements identifying revenue distribution across Pabili, Padala, and Bills Payment categories with visualization of peak demand hours and seasonal trends.
ii)	Rider Performance Reports – Listing all thirty-eight active riders ranked by completed errand count for any specified time period, successful delivery rate calculated as percentage of completed errands against total assignments, average response time from dispatch notification to rider acceptance, average completion time from assignment to delivery confirmation, customer rating aggregations when feedback features are implemented, frequency of payment disputes or delivery incidents, and identification of top-performing riders eligible for incentive bonuses.
iii)	The Transaction Settlement Report  - Enables the manager to record the total business capital deployed for customer purchases throughout the day, automatically calculates the commission earnings using the configured ten percent or fifty peso rules, applies the 25 peso multi-store surcharge for each additional pickup location, generates the final Sugo Share representing business revenue after rider payments, and produces the Rider Share showing total earnings from base delivery fees and applicable incentives.
iv)	Transaction Summary Reports – Producing automated daily summaries that eliminate physical receipt cross-referencing, itemizing every transaction with rider name, customer location, purchase amount, service fee applied, commission earned, and payment method collected, calculating total Sugo Share as business revenue from commissions and surcharges, calculating total Rider Share from base delivery fees, reconciling cash collected against capital deployed for purchases, and providing downloadable PDF statements for accounting records and tax documentation.
v)	Commission Reports – Providing exhaustive breakdown of grocery errand calculations, distinguishing between transactions qualifying for ten percent variable commission versus 50 pesos flat fee, listing all multiple store surcharges with store count per errand, summarizing commission revenue by individual rider, identifying high-value grocery transactions exceeding one thousand pesos, and tracking commission trends across weekly and monthly periods for rate optimization analysis.
2)	Frontend Modules
a)	The Errand Transaction Management Module - Handles all functionality necessary for riders including viewing the digital job queue showing available assignments with estimated earnings, accepting or declining specific errands with automated notification to dispatchers, viewing digital waybills containing complete customer information and structured landmark descriptions, updating progress status through defined stages including traveling to pick up, arrived at store, purchasing items, traveling to delivery, and delivered, recording actual purchase amounts for reimbursement and commission calculation, capturing photographs of original store receipts with automatic upload and linking to transaction records, capturing photographs of purchased items for customer verification, recording payment collection with amount verification and method specification, and marking errands as complete with timestamp and geolocation confirmation.
b)	The Rate Display Module - Shows riders the automatically calculated fee breakdown for each errand including base delivery fee based on distance, itemized list of stores with 25-peso surcharge indicators, running subtotal of purchase costs, applied commission amount using the ten percent or 50 peso, and final total amount to collect from customer or confirm for digital payments.
c)	Customer-self Service Module – Shows the menu in the app interface system providing a choice of available and unavailable orders to do an errand task in the app. Moreover, to navigate the user when is the location and status of the rider in order to provide more details on the task that has been made.
